### Typography Rules

**Line Height (Leading):**
- Headlines: 1.2–1.3x font size
- Body text: 1.5–1.6x font size
- Minimum for accessibility: 1.4x

**Letter Spacing (Tracking):**
- Headlines: -0.02em to 0em (slightly tighter)
- Body text: 0em (default)
- All caps: +0.05em to +0.1em (slightly looser)

**Paragraph Spacing:**
- Between paragraphs: 1em to 1.5em
- Between sections: 2em to 3em

**Line Length:**
- Optimal: 50–75 characters per line
- Maximum: 90 characters per line

**Alignment:**
- Body text: Left-aligned (for left-to-right languages)
- Headlines: Left or center (contextual)
- Avoid: Justified text (creates uneven spacing)

## Brand Voice & Tone

### Our Voice

Our brand voice is the consistent personality that comes through in everything we write. It doesn't change.

**Our voice is:**
[3-5 descriptive attributes]

**Voice Description:**
[2-3 paragraphs describing your brand voice]

**Example:**
*Our voice is confident yet approachable, professional yet personable. We speak with authority on our subject matter while remaining accessible to all audiences. We're the knowledgeable friend who explains complex topics in simple terms, never talking down to our audience but never assuming they know industry jargon.*

### Our Tone

While voice remains consistent, tone adapts to context:

#### Tone by Context

**Marketing & Sales:**
- Enthusiastic and inspiring
- Benefit-focused
- Future-oriented
- Example: "Transform your business with technology that actually works for you."

**Customer Support:**
- Patient and empathetic
- Solution-oriented
- Reassuring
- Example: "I understand how frustrating this must be. Let's solve this together."

**Technical Documentation:**
- Clear and precise
- Instructive
- Methodical
- Example: "Follow these three steps to configure your settings."

**Social Media:**
- Conversational and engaging
- Timely and relevant
- Slightly casual
- Example: "Great question! Here's what we recommend..."

**Crisis/Serious Issues:**
- Honest and transparent
- Calm and professional
- Accountable
- Example: "We take this seriously and are actively addressing the issue."

### Writing Guidelines

**DO:**
- ✓ Use active voice ("We built" not "It was built")
- ✓ Write in second person ("You can" not "Users can")
- ✓ Be concise and direct
- ✓ Use contractions occasionally (we're, you'll, can't)
- ✓ Use simple, clear language
- ✓ Break up long paragraphs
- ✓ Use bullet points for lists
- ✓ Write like you speak
- ✓ Focus on benefits, not features

**DON'T:**
- ✗ Use jargon without explanation
- ✗ Be overly formal or corporate-speak
- ✗ Use clichés or buzzwords (synergy, leverage, paradigm shift)
- ✗ Write in passive voice
- ✗ Use all caps (except acronyms)
- ✗ Be vague or ambiguous
- ✗ Overuse exclamation points!!!
- ✗ Assume too much knowledge

## Appendix

### Glossary

**Accessibility:** Design practice ensuring content is usable by people of all abilities

**Bleed:** Area extending beyond trim edge in print design (typically 0.125")

**CMYK:** Color mode for print (Cyan, Magenta, Yellow, Black)

**Contrast Ratio:** Measurement of color contrast for accessibility (e.g., 4.5:1)

**DPI:** Dots per inch - resolution measurement for print

**Hex Color:** Six-digit code representing colors in web design (e.g., #1E40AF)

**Kerning:** Space between individual letter pairs

**Leading:** Vertical space between lines of text (line height)

**Pantone:** Standardized color matching system for print

**RGB:** Color mode for digital displays (Red, Green, Blue)

**Sans-serif:** Font without decorative strokes (e.g., Arial, Helvetica)

**Serif:** Font with decorative strokes at ends of letters (e.g., Times, Georgia)

**Tracking:** Overall spacing between all letters in text

**WCAG:** Web Content Accessibility Guidelines - standards for accessible design
