# Best Practices by Medium

This guide covers best practices for different communication channels and mediums.

## Email Communications

### When to Use
- Formal announcements
- Policy changes
- Detailed explanations
- External stakeholder communications
- Archival importance
- Need for receipts/records

### Best Practices
- Subject line: Clear, specific, action-oriented
- Keep under 500 words when possible
- Use bullet points and formatting
- Put most important info first
- Include clear next steps
- Send at optimal times (Tues-Thurs, 10am-2pm)
- Avoid Reply All when possible
- Use BCC for large lists
- Provide opt-out for non-essential lists

### Subject Line Formulas
- Action-oriented: "[Action Required] Submit expenses by Friday"
- Update: "[Update] Q4 Planning Schedule"
- FYI: "[FYI] New parking policy effective 1/1"
- Question: "[Input Needed] Feedback on office hours"

## Slack/Chat Communications

### When to Use
- Quick updates
- Team coordination
- Informal announcements
- Real-time collaboration
- Building culture
- Quick questions

### Best Practices
- Use threads to keep channels organized
- Post in appropriate channel (not DM for team info)
- Use @here and @channel sparingly
- Include context in your message
- Use emoji for tone and reactions
- Pin important messages
- Set reminders for follow-ups
- Use Slack workflows for recurring updates

### Channel Organization
- #announcements: Company-wide important news
- #general: General discussion, team bonding
- #wins: Celebrating achievements
- #team-[name]: Team-specific updates
- #project-[name]: Project coordination
- #help-[topic]: Getting assistance

## Wiki/Intranet Documentation

### When to Use
- Long-term reference material
- Policies and procedures
- Onboarding information
- Team directories
- Living documents
- How-to guides

### Best Practices
- Create clear information architecture
- Use templates for consistency
- Include table of contents
- Add last updated date
- Link related documents
- Make it searchable
- Regular audits for outdated info
- Clear ownership for each page

### Essential Pages
- Company handbook
- Team org charts and contacts
- Communication guidelines
- Common FAQs
- Process documentation
- Project archives
- Meeting notes

## Meeting-Based Communications

### When to Use
- Complex topics requiring discussion
- Change management
- Team alignment
- Brainstorming and collaboration
- Sensitive topics
- Building relationships

### Best Practices
- Send agenda in advance
- Start with context and objectives
- Leave time for questions
- Take and distribute notes
- Record for those who can't attend
- End with clear action items
- Follow up with written summary

### All-Hands Meeting Structure
- (5 min) Company metrics and progress
- (10 min) Major announcements
- (10 min) Team spotlights
- (10 min) Q&A
- (5 min) Recognitions and fun

## Video/Recorded Communications

### When to Use
- Distributed/async teams
- Complex demonstrations
- Personal touch from leadership
- Training and onboarding
- Product updates with visuals

### Best Practices
- Keep videos under 5 minutes when possible
- Use captions for accessibility
- Provide written summary
- Make searchable/indexed
- Use good audio quality
- Include timestamps in description
- Store in accessible location
